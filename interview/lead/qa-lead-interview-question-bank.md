# QA Lead Interview Question Bank

## What

Structured question set for lead-level AQA/SDET interviews covering framework architecture,
delivery process, CI/CD, UI/mobile troubleshooting, and engineering depth.

## Why

Lead interviews require both technical depth and decision-making rationale.
A reusable bank helps evaluate consistency of candidate thinking and communication.

## How

### Core evaluation areas

- automation strategy and ROI;
- framework architecture and modularity;
- design patterns in UI/API/DB automation;
- test runtime optimization and stability;
- CI/CD integration and debugging practices;
- mentoring and incident ownership behavior.

### Example lead questions

1. How do you decide what to automate first?
2. What modules are mandatory in a scalable test framework?
3. Which design patterns have you applied in production test code?
4. How do you reduce runtime without losing confidence?
5. How do you organize mobile/web test debugging workflow?
6. How do you connect dependent jobs in CI pipelines?

### Scoring rubric (1-5)

- **1-2**: generic theory, weak practical examples;
- **3**: correct basics, limited system-level thinking;
- **4**: strong applied examples, clear trade-offs;
- **5**: architecture-level ownership, measurable outcomes, mentoring impact.

## Interview Q&A Starters

**Q1: What separates senior from lead in AQA?**  
Lead defines strategy, quality gates, and organizational execution, not only writes tests.

**Q2: Which anti-pattern appears most often in automation teams?**  
Growing test volume without ownership model, runtime budgets, and flakiness governance.

**Q3: How do you demonstrate impact in interview?**  
Show concrete improvements: runtime reduction, defect escape decrease, onboarding speedup.
